HTTP/2, HTTP/3 and HTTPS

HTTP/2 (2015) introduced multiplexing to send multiple requests at once[cite: 299, 300]. HTTP/3 uses QUIC for even faster connections[cite: 306, 307]. HTTPS adds a layer of TLS encryption to keep data secure from attackers[cite: 323, 324].
