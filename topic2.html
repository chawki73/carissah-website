Modern Protocols and Security

HTTP/2 and HTTP/3

HTTP/2 was a game-changer in 2015, introducing multiplexing—the ability to send multiple requests at once [cite: 299-300]. Today, HTTP/3 is pushing speed even further by using the QUIC protocol, which runs over UDP for faster connections on mobile networks [cite: 305-307].

The Importance of HTTPS

Security is no longer optional. HTTPS uses TLS encryption to ensure that data intercepted by attackers cannot be read or altered [cite: 323-324]. Major browsers now label plain HTTP sites as "Not Secure" to protect users[cite: 327].
